How this project meets the assignment

The webpage shows two real satellite-derived visuals that update from NASA Earthdata services as new imagery becomes available.

Soil moisture

The left map uses SMAP, NASA's Soil Moisture Active Passive mission. SMAP measures moisture in the upper layer of soil across Earth, which helps scientists track drought, flooding risk, and crop conditions.

Green cover

The right map uses MODIS NDVI imagery from the Terra satellite. NDVI estimates how green and active vegetation is by comparing reflected light from plant-covered land.

Selected country or grid area

Users can either zoom to a full country or enter a custom latitude and longitude box, which makes the site match the assignment requirement to study a selected country or grid area on Earth.

Real-time updating

This site refreshes its maps on a timer and pulls the latest available NASA GIBS visual layers. The data is near real time rather than second-by-second live video, because satellite products are published after each processing cycle.
