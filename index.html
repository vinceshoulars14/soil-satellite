What the colors mean

Soil moisture metric scale

NASA GIBS reports the moisture colors in volumetric water content (m3/m3). Yellow-orange indicates drier soil, while lighter to deeper blue marks wetter soil. The same legend is used for both the 0-5 cm surface layer and the 0-100 cm root-zone layer.

Soil moisture uncertainty

Lower uncertainty means the moisture estimate is more stable for that area and time step. Higher values flag places where the modeled soil moisture estimate deserves more caution.

Green cover (NDVI)

Sparse vegetation trends toward tan. Dense green vegetation trends toward deep green.

Data sources

Soil moisture uses NASA Earthdata GIBS layer SMAP_L4_Analyzed_Surface_Soil_Moisture.

Added SMAP comparison layers include SMAP_L4_Analyzed_Root_Zone_Soil_Moisture, SMAP_L4_Uncertainty_Analyzed_Surface_Soil_Moisture, and SMAP_L4_Uncertainty_Analyzed_Root_Zone_Soil_Moisture.

Green cover uses NASA Earthdata GIBS layer MODIS_Terra_L3_NDVI_16Day.

Country boundaries are loaded from a public GeoJSON file for zooming and selection.
